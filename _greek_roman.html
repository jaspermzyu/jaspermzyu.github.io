The Three Fates(Fates Parcae) are divine beings that preside over the fates of every god, giant, titan and mortal. The three fates and their jobs are:

  1. Clotho(Nona) weaved the Threads of Fate, the beginning of all life.
  2. Lahkesis(Decima) would measeure the threads, determining how long a mortal, God, giants and Titans life span would be.
  3. Atropos(Morta) cuts the threads, ending the life

Egyptian Mythology

Egyptian mythology believed in many different gods

  1. Ra(Atum, Khepri and Khnum):God of the sun, heaven, kingship, power, and light
  2. Osiris: God of the underworld
  3. Isis: Goddess of love, healing, fertility, magic, and the moon
  4. Horus: God of kingship, healing, protection, the sun, and the sky
  5. Set: God of war, chaos and storms
  6. Ptah: God of creation
  7. Nekhbet: Goddess of vultures and protector of Upper Egypt
  8. Babi: God of baboons and virtility of the dead
  9. Thoth: God of the moon, of reckoning, of learning, and of writing
  10. Shu: God of the air and wind
  11. Nut: Goddess of the sky, stars, cosmos, mothers, astronomy, and the universe
  12. Geb: God of the Earth
  13. Khonsu: God of the moon
  14. Nephthys: Goddess of death, decay and darkness
  15. Anubis: God of funerary practices and care of the dead
  16. Taweret: Goddess of childbirth
  17. Bes: Dwarf God of music, merriment, and childbirth
  18. Hathor: Goddess of love, beauty, music, dancing, fertility, and pleasure
  19. Sekhmet: Goddess of the hot desert sun, plague, chaos, war, and healing
  20. Bastet: Goddess of protection, pleasure, and the bringer of good health
  21. Wadjet: Goddess of serpents, the Nile Delta, the land of the living, and protector of Egyptian kings and protector of Lower Egypt
  22. Heket: Goddess of frogs and fertility
  23. Maahes: God of lions and war
  24. Sobek: God of crocidiles and creator of the Nile river
  25. Serqet: Goddess of scorpions
  26. Aphophis: God of Chaos
