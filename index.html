Cyclones, Hurricanes and Typhoons

Cyclones

Cyclones are tropical storms with winds blowing in a large spiral around the center which is known as the eye which is like a calm zone. Cyclone Jasper's maximum wind speed was 220 km an hour. A cyclone is usually formed in areas with low pressure which draws in nearby winds as the Earth rotates in creates forces the cause the winds to swirl around the low pressure which helps the cyclone to start to spin.

Hurricanes

Hurricanes are a rapidly rotating storm system formed by low pressure points with strong winds. For example: Hurricane Patricia is the fastest hurricane ever recorded with winds hitting top speeds of 345 km an hour. Hurricanes are a spiral arrangement of thunderstorms that can produce heavy rain. Warm ocean waters and thunderstorms fuel power-hungry hurricanes. Hurricanes form over the ocean, often beginning as a tropical waveâ€”a low pressure area that moves through the moisture-rich tropics, possibly enhancing shower and thunderstorm activity.

Typhoons

Typhoons are formed when wind blows into areas of the ocean where the water is warm. The wind collects the moisture and starts to rise while the colder air moves below which causes pressure which causes the winds to move very quickly. The winds rotate or spin around the center known as the eye. Typhoons are cyclones that originate over warm tropical oceans. The most powerful Typhoon was Typhoon Haiyan. Typhoons can also sometimes make fish rain from the sky since the Typhoons can catch fish from the ocean.

Earthquakes are catastrophic events that happen when two tectonic plates collide resulting in the earth to crack. Earthquake usually happen in Japan because Japan is between 4 tectonic plates and when one collides to another it causes an earth either in Japan or in the water near Japan resulting in a tsunami. The most powerful Earthquake: The Indian Ocean Earthquake in 2004 resulted in a devastating 277,898 deaths. Japan has developed houses that have a lighter roof as well as having the ability to sway around when there is an earthquake so that the main building doesn't collapse. Over 80% of large earthquakes occcur aroudnt the edges of the Pacific ocean, an area slo known as the "Ring Of Fire". The Ring of fire is full of volcanoes that when one volcano erupts slowly every single volcano in the Ring of fire would erupt as well as creating absolutely devastating earthquakes. The Ring of fire has existed for more than 35 million years. The Ring of fire has minor and major eruptions occuring about every 50 years.

Bushfires

Bushfires are natural fires that spark up and can help plants reproduce since some plants have adapted to use fire as part of their survival for example: The bottlebrush plant has adapted so that when there is a bushfire it the burn the outer layer of the plants head and it releases the seeds stored inside the plant which grows into more bottlebrush plant and the cycle continues. Bushfires can also help the areas that are burned because it kills insects and diseases that could be harming the trees in the area. The fire can also make room for new shoots to grow as well as new habitats for the wildlife. However bushfires can still be dangerous because if a bushfire sparks near a community the people living in the community will be forced to evacuate and if there is a farm in the area it would kill the livestock in the farm as well as destroying crops. For example: The Black Saturday Fires was the worst bushfire in Australiaâ€™s modern history. It happened at the 7th of February 2009 in Victoria and it killed 173 people and injured 414 and demolished ove 3,500 buildings including 2,029 houses and 7,562 people were forced to evacuate. People who protect areas can do a controlled burn since it can help the plants. A controlled burn is done by removing possible fuel from the area so that the fire wouldnâ€™t get absolutely massive.

Volcanoes

Volcanoes are formed when one tectonic moves under another tectonic plate. Usually a thin, heavy oceanic plate subducts or moves under a thick contintental plate. Volcanoes have a place under the earths crust called a magma chamber which is where the magma is kept. The place where the magma can come out of is called a vent. Volcanoes can erupt since magma is lighter than the solid rock around it, magma rises and collects in the magma chambers. Eventually some of the magma pushes through vents and fissure to the surface. When magma reaches the surface it is called lava instead of magma. The possibly most famous volcanic eruption was the eruption of Mount Vesuvius in 1944. It when pompeii fell to the volcanic ash and lava of the humongous stratovolcano. This eruption killed about 16,000 people in total but it only killed 2,000 people in pompeii. The largest volcano we know of is on Mars called Olympus Mons. Olympus Mons is a shield volcano which means it eruptions aren't very deadly.
